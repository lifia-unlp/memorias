Editorial & Background

About the Memorias Portal

The Memorias Portal is an academic and administrative platform designed specifically to document, curate, and showcase the scientific production of research laboratories. Originally developed for LIFIA (Laboratorio de Investigación y Formación en Informática Avanzada), the portal integrates all active research parameters to create a unified public ledger of academic milestones.

By mapping the co-dependent relations between researchers, active funding grants, academic theses, doctoral scholarships, and peer-reviewed publications, the portal allows students, investigators, and external evaluators to audit scientific efficiency in real-time.

The Transition: Dropping Tailwind in favor of MUI

While Tailwind CSS is excellent for highly custom, utility-first interfaces, scientific and enterprise portals often benefit from high-level standardization. The migration of the Memorias codebase from Tailwind to Material-UI (MUI) was driven by several key factors:

  • Design Consistency: MUI implements strict Material Design guidelines, ensuring that dense academic views, forms, and statistics tables share uniform margins, colors, and elevations.
  • Form Curation Speed: Built-in MUI fields, auto-fills, and selectors decrease boilerplate code for complex creation/edit forms.
  • Accessiblity Standards: Native MUI elements inherit WAI-ARIA compliance, raising accessibility for external evaluators using assistive tech.
  • Maintained Aesthetics: Pre-configured light/dark color tokens allow developers to concentrate on domain features rather than custom CSS styling overrides.

Domain Entity Schemas

Memorias revolves around five central entity models. Their relationships are visually defined as follows:

👥

Members (Investigadores)

The laboratory researchers (directors, co-directors, fellows, students) affiliated with CONICET, CIC, UNLP, or other agencies.

💼

Projects (Proyectos de Investigación)

Active R&D grants sponsored by national agencies (PICT, UNLP, CIC). They link researchers to specific roles (Director, Team Member).

🎓

Theses (Tesis Académicas)

Doctoral or undergraduate dissertations conducted in the lab. Tracks completion progress (0-100%) and supervisory relationships.

🎟️

Scholarships (Becas)

Doctoral or postdoc training fellowships funded by CONICET/CIC. Maps fellow supervisors and agency start/end periods.

📚

Publications (Bibliografía)

Peer-reviewed journals, conference papers, or book chapters written by lab members. Includes copyable citations and BibTeX records.
