Academic Thesis Abstract

Semantic Curation Frameworks for Heterogeneous Academic Publications

This thesis addresses structural curation issues within modern university repositories and laboratory indexes. Academic portals today must process a heterogeneous volume of bibliography entries originating from multiple databases (IEEE, ACM, Springer) which rarely match in citation schemas or metadata quality.

By introducing a decentralized Knowledge Graph framework, we propose automated semantic alignment pipelines. The framework ingests raw BibTeX metadata, maps it to a unified ontology scale, resolves duplicate authors through custom disambiguation heuristic weights, and produces rich, curated citations supporting interactive search models.

Defense Information & Laboratory Context

This thesis was successfully defended in front of an academic jury at the Faculty of Informatics, National University of La Plata (UNLP), on March 15, 2025, satisfying all conditions to award the student the degree of Doctor in Computer Science.
